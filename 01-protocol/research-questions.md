# Research Questions & PCC Mapping

## PCC (Population, Concept, Context) Mapping
This review uses the PCC framework to define the boundaries of the architectural study on rule-based systems.

| Element | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Population (P)** | Rule-Based Inference Engines and Knowledge-Based Systems (KBS). |
| **Concept (C)** | Scalable, modular, and distributed inference architectures (SRBIA). |
| **Context (C)** | Enterprise-grade systems with large rule bases requiring high performance, maintainability, and explainability. |

## Research Questions

**RQ1.** What techniques and algorithms (e.g., Rete, Forward/Backward chaining) are used in existing rule-based inference systems to handle logic?

**RQ2.** What architectural patterns (Monolithic vs. Distributed/Layered) are employed in scalable KBS to manage rule explosion?

**RQ3.** What are the key limitations (bottlenecks) of current rule-based systems regarding scalability and modularity?

**RQ4.** How do existing systems handle knowledge representation to balance reasoning efficiency with system performance?
